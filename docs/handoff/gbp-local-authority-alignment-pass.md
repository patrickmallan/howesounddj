## 4. GBP recommendations

**Note:** Verify categories available in your GBP account UI; names vary slightly by region.

### Primary category (recommendation)

- **Wedding service** or **DJ** (whichever Google offers that best matches “wedding DJ” as primary intent).  
- If both exist and “DJ” reads too nightclub-forward, **Wedding service** often aligns better with **wedding buyer** intent; if “Mobile DJ” is the only strong match, use it and lean secondary categories for refinement.

### Secondary categories (recommendations)

- **Event planner** (only if you genuinely offer planning-adjacent coordination—otherwise skip).
- **Entertainment agency** — usually **no**, unless accurate.
- Prefer **few accurate secondaries** over many weak ones.

Practical pairings that often fit wedding-DJ reality:

- Primary: **Wedding service** (or **DJ**)  
- Secondary: **Party equipment rental service** — only if you rent gear as a business line; else omit.

**Action:** Pick secondaries that reflect **verified** business operations; do not claim rental or planning categories without substance.

### Service area (recommendations)

- **Primary location:** Squamish (GBP address or service-area business, per how Patrick operates).
- **Service areas:** Define a **tight list** matching real travel: e.g. Squamish, Whistler, Pemberton, corridor communities, and **select** Lower Mainland areas if you routinely serve Vancouver-origin weddings **at** Sea-to-Sky venues—not “all of BC.”
- **Consistency:** Service-area list should **echo** site language: Squamish-rooted, Sea-to-Sky corridor, Whistler, Vancouver as **planning origin**—not as a fake Vancouver storefront.

### GBP description alignment (recommendations)

Mirror site **positioning**, not site **keywords**:

- Open with **who + where + what**: Squamish-based wedding DJ; Sea-to-Sky and mountain weddings; ceremony through reception.
- Mention **calm planning**, **atmosphere-first**, **personalized music**—aligned with on-site tone.
- Avoid superlatives (“#1,” “best”), fake awards, and **venue partnership** claims unless contractually true and allowed.

### Photo strategy (recommendations)

- **Owner / at-work**: Patrick at decks or in planning context (authentic, not stock).
- **Regional context**: tasteful corridor/mountain **environment** shots that do not imply a **specific private wedding** without permission.
- **Equipment / setup**: discrete rig photos that signal **professionalism** without clutter.
- **Couple photos**: only **licensed / permitted** images; never imply a venue endorsement from a photo alone.

### GBP post strategy (recommendations)

- **Low frequency, high quality**: monthly or biweekly is enough for a premium brand.
- Topics: planning tips aligned with **guides** (link to site), **seasonal** corridor notes (weather, timing), **story** launches—never fake events.
- Tone: calm, editorial—match site voice.

### Review acquisition (recommendations)

- **Ethical asks**: one gentle post-event follow-up with a **direct link** to GBP/Google review flow (and only when couples are genuinely happy).
- **Never** incentivize reviews or cherry-pick against Google policies.
- **Diversity of location cues** in reviews (Squamish, Whistler, Sea-to-Sky) reinforces map relevance **organically**—do not coach reviewers to stuff keywords.

### Venue / geographic reinforcement (on GBP)

- Do not list **“preferred vendor”** unless venues confirm and policy allows.
- In **Q&A**, answer logistics questions honestly (coverage area, ceremony audio, travel).

### Local authority reinforcement (on GBP)

- **Services / attributes:** wedding reception, ceremony audio—only if true.
- **Products:** avoid turning GBP into a package grid; keep **consultation + wedding DJ** clarity.
