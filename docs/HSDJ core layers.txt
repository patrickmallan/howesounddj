1. SEARCH VISIBILITY (Can people even find you?)

What I’m grading
	•	Keyword alignment (e.g. “Squamish wedding DJ”)
	•	Page targeting (1 intent = 1 page)
	•	Title tags + meta descriptions (CTR quality, not just keywords)
	•	Local SEO signals
	•	Internal linking structure

What “high score” looks like
	•	You clearly win one primary keyword per page
	•	Homepage = broad intent (“Squamish Wedding DJ”)
	•	Supporting pages = deeper intent (packages, venues, FAQs)
	•	Google instantly understands:
	•	what you do
	•	where you do it
	•	who you serve

What kills scores
	•	Generic titles like “Home”
	•	Trying to rank one page for everything
	•	No local modifiers (Squamish / Sea-to-Sky)

⸻

2. MESSAGE CLARITY (Do people understand you in 3 seconds?)

What I’m grading
	•	Hero section (above the fold)
	•	Immediate value proposition
	•	Differentiation
	•	Audience specificity

What “high score” looks like

Within 3 seconds, a bride/groom knows:
	•	“This is for weddings”
	•	“This is in my area”
	•	“This feels premium / fun / reliable”
	•	“This is different from the others”

Example structure:
	•	Headline: outcome
	•	Subheadline: who + where + vibe
	•	CTA: obvious next step

What kills scores
	•	Vague copy (“creating unforgettable moments”)
	•	No location clarity
	•	No personality

⸻

3. TRUST & AUTHORITY (Do I believe you’re the right choice?)

What I’m grading
	•	Reviews / testimonials
	•	Social proof density and placement
	•	Visual credibility (photos, real weddings)
	•	Authority signals (experience, venues, partnerships)

What “high score” looks like
	•	Reviews appear early (above the fold or shortly after)
	•	Real names, real couples, real photos
	•	Proof that:
	•	you’ve done this many times
	•	people loved it
	•	you handle weddings specifically

What kills scores
	•	Reviews buried at the bottom
	•	No photos of actual events
	•	Generic testimonials

⸻

4. CONVERSION ENGINE (Do people take action?)

What I’m grading
	•	CTA clarity and frequency
	•	Friction in the inquiry process
	•	Form UX
	•	Emotional momentum toward action

What “high score” looks like
	•	Clear primary CTA: “Check Availability”
	•	Repeated at key scroll points
	•	Form is:
	•	short
	•	clear
	•	mobile-friendly
	•	“What happens next” is explained

What kills scores
	•	Too many CTAs competing
	•	Long forms
	•	No reassurance after submission
	•	Weak CTA language (“Contact us”)

⸻

5. USER EXPERIENCE (Does it feel smooth and premium?)

What I’m grading
	•	Mobile experience (this is huge for weddings)
	•	Load speed (especially hero)
	•	Layout flow and readability
	•	Visual hierarchy

What “high score” looks like
	•	Fast load, especially above-the-fold
	•	Clean spacing, easy scanning
	•	Buttons easy to tap on mobile
	•	No friction or confusion

What kills scores
	•	Slow hero images
	•	Cluttered layout
	•	Tiny buttons / bad mobile UX

⸻

6. BRAND POSITIONING (Why YOU?)

What I’m grading
	•	Differentiation vs other DJs
	•	Emotional resonance
	•	Tone consistency

What “high score” looks like

You are not interchangeable.

Examples:
	•	“Bangers only”
	•	“No cheesy MC energy”
	•	“Packed dance floors, every time”

What kills scores
	•	Sounding like every other DJ
	•	Playing it safe
	•	No clear personality

⸻

7. CONTENT DEPTH (Do you answer real questions?)

What I’m grading
	•	FAQ quality
	•	Objection handling
	•	Decision-stage content

What “high score” looks like

You answer:
	•	“What does a wedding DJ actually do?”
	•	“How do you read the room?”
	•	“What happens if something goes wrong?”
	•	“How do we plan music?”

What kills scores
	•	Surface-level FAQs
	•	No objection handling

⸻

8. ANALYTICS & FEEDBACK LOOP (Are you learning?)

What I’m grading
	•	Event tracking
	•	Conversion tracking
	•	Data clarity

What “high score” looks like

You know:
	•	where users drop off
	•	which pages convert
	•	which CTAs work

What kills scores
	•	No tracking
	•	Guessing instead of measuring

⸻

9. OFFER STRUCTURE (Is it easy to say yes?)

What I’m grading
	•	Package clarity
	•	Pricing transparency (or intentional opacity)
	•	Perceived value

What “high score” looks like
	•	Simple, clear options
	•	Value is obvious
	•	No confusion about what you get

What kills scores
	•	Overcomplicated packages
	•	Hidden or unclear value

⸻

10. LOCAL DOMINANCE (Do you “own” your area?)

What I’m grading
	•	Location authority signals
	•	Venue familiarity
	•	Regional language

What “high score” looks like
	•	Mentions of:
	•	Squamish
	•	Sea-to-Sky
	•	specific venues
	•	Feels local, not generic

What kills scores
	•	Generic “we serve everywhere”
	•	No local proof

The Scoring Reality (What actually matters most)

If I compress this to what actually drives bookings, it’s this:
	1.	Clarity (what you do + who for + where)
	2.	Trust (reviews + proof)
	3.	CTA + form friction
	4.	Mobile UX
	5.	Local SEO
